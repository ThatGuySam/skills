# Local Paid-Quote Adjustment

Use this adjustment when the prompt points to a local vendor, community event, nonprofit organizer, or relationship-priced work.

## Purpose

Public, retail, or corporate-market anchors can be too high when the decision is about what a small local organizer will actually pay. Local nonprofit/community work may include discounted pricing, simplified scope, pickup/local handling, bundling, waived setup, or goodwill pricing.

## Workflow

1. Write down the full-market scenario first so the anchor is explicit.
2. Write down at least one paid-quote scenario that could reduce the actual cost:
   - nonprofit or community discount
   - friendly local-vendor pricing
   - simplified deliverables
   - pickup/local handling instead of shipping or full service
   - bundling, waived setup, or light sponsorship goodwill
3. Decide which quantity the prompt asks for:
   - `paid cost` or `invoice paid`: emphasize the scenario the organizer is most likely to actually pay
   - `vendor quote`: emphasize the quoted amount in this local context, not an abstract national list price
4. Set the central estimate as a weighted blend, leaning toward the paid-quote scenario when local nonprofit/community context is explicit.
5. Stress-test the low bound. If a realistic discounted local quote would fall below it, lower the bound or widen the interval.

## Calibration Notes

- Media, apparel, signage, and custom local-vendor work can show larger nonprofit/community discounts than small SaaS or commodity rentals.
- Small SaaS, security, and minor rentals may already be close to public pricing; apply a lighter adjustment there unless the prompt suggests otherwise.
- Do not force every estimate downward. The goal is to represent the plausible paid range, not assume every vendor discounts.
